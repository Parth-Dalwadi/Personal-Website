2. Gintama

Gintama is easily the funniest show I've ever watched while still being able to have a dire and emotional plot; I would even go so far as to say that it's probably unparalleled in this aspect. There's genuinely nothing that I don't like about this show. I was eager to watch every episode, which speaks volumes about my enjoyment as Gintama has a total of 367.


In the beginning, it can be boring for some as it introduces you to numerous characters essential for the story through comedic episodes that don't connect to one another. Personally, I had no problems with this and, in fact, enjoyed it a lot as I found it incredibly entertaining and because I liked all of the characters. I think that for anyone opposed to this, you should understand that this show isn't like Spongebob with no overarching plot. There will be a point where the episodes are less episodic and start to have actual story arcs that build the world and characters up even more. These early episodes are essential for Gintama to set the stage for its impeccable story arcs down the road.
